Academics

Much is expected of Chrysalis students from an academic perspective. They are required to be diligent, focused, self-starters who will maximise every moment of their academic day and take their academics very seriously. Students who fail to satisfactorily complete all the required prescribed work will not be entered for GED or Cambridge Final Examinations. While the environment is nurturing and classes are very small (10-15 in a class), students are reminded that Cambridge is one of the most difficult curricula in the world today and that Chrysalis Private School is not a suitable choice for a student seeking an “easy way” out of academics.

To date, Chrysalis students have received excellent symbols with 90% of our students achieving distinctions. Nevertheless, parents acknowledge that the full and final responsibility of the education of their child rests with them.

A Cambridge or GED Matric is accepted all over the world, whereas a South African Matric is not. Chrysalis Private School students are therefore given every advantage to access international tuition and training.
